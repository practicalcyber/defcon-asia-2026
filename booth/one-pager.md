## Why we're here

Most teams have built systems where the **AI is the deterministic layer** and the **deterministic code is the AI edge**. That's backwards. Today, we're flipping it.

> **The 98/2 Principle**
> 98% hardened, deterministic, auditable code.
> 2% intelligent AI edge.
> The critical logic must be defendable in court. The AI makes the experience feel magical.

---

## Three Missions — pick one, build it today

### 🛡️ Mission 1 — The Signal Extractor
**SecOps & Analysis.** Build a pipeline that ingests messy security data (cloud trail logs, memory dump, proxy traffic) and produces an auditable dashboard. Deterministic logic strips the 99% of known-good noise. AI narrates the *vibe* of what's left. The "what was ignored" must be hard-coded. The "why this is suspicious" can be AI-generated.

### 🎯 Mission 2 — The Stealth Architect
**Offensive Security.** Use AI agents to architect and deploy hardened offensive infrastructure — multi-tier redirector, localised phishing payload factory, custom C2 backend. AI does the design. Encryption, IP whitelisting, and access control stay 100% deterministic code.

### 💻 Mission 3 — The TDD Speedrun
**Software Engineering.** Concept → production-ready security tool in **30 minutes**. You write the tests. AI writes the implementation. If the AI hallucinates logic that breaks a test, you architect the fix. Target: 90%+ coverage, full docs, working binary.

## Quick glossary

- **LLM** — a statistical machine that predicts the next token of text. It does not "know"; it patterns.
- **Prompt injection** — slipping instructions into data the model reads, so the model executes the attacker's intent.
- **RAG** — Retrieval-Augmented Generation. The model reads documents fetched from a store before answering. Looks like search; behaves like execution.
- **Alignment** — the model's learned tendency to refuse unsafe requests. A tendency, not a rule. Tendencies bend.
- **Vibe coding** — building software by spec + AI codegen + human rejection. The discipline is the rejection.

---

## Two ideas to take home

1. **Intellectual labour vs accountability labour.** AI does the intellectual labour — drafting, summarising, pattern-matching. Humans do the accountability labour — the decision and the defence of it when something breaks. Human-in-the-Loop isn't a safety net; it's a structural division of labour.

2. **The Translator capability.** You don't need to become an ML engineer. You need Minimum Viable Literacy: enough to commission, interrogate, and challenge expert advice. The leader who can't be fooled.
